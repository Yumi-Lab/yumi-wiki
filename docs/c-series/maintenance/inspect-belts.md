# Inspect Belts

**Interval:** Every 90 days  
**Difficulty:** Medium  
**Time:** 10 minutes  
**Applies to:** C235, C335, C435

## Why

Belt tension directly affects print quality. Loose belts cause:

- Ringing/ghosting artifacts
- Dimensional inaccuracy
- Layer shifts at high speeds

## What You Need

- No special tools (visual and tactile inspection)
- Input shaper results for comparison (optional)

## Steps

### 1. Power off the printer
Turn off the printer and unplug it.

### 2. Check X belt tension
Press the X belt with your finger at the midpoint. It should deflect 1-2mm with moderate pressure and feel firm like a guitar string.

### 3. Check Y belt tension
Same test on the Y belt(s). On C Series, there are two Y belts — check both.

### 4. Check for wear
Look for:

- Frayed edges
- Missing teeth
- Cracks or glazing on the tooth side
- Dust accumulation between teeth

### 5. Compare with input shaper
If you've run input shaper before, compare the resonance frequencies. A drop of more than 5 Hz from the baseline suggests the belt has loosened.

### 6. Adjust if needed
If belts are loose, tighten the belt tensioners. Turn the tensioner screw 1/4 turn at a time and recheck.

### 7. Mark as done
On your SmartPad: **Menu > More > Maintenance > Inspect Belts > Done**

## Tips

- New belts stretch in the first 50 hours — recheck after initial break-in
- If a belt breaks, replace both X/Y belts at the same time
- Run input shaper after re-tensioning to verify
